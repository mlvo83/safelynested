Onboarding a Charity Facilitator

A guide for charity primary contacts adding a facilitator to their team
What this guide covers. The full process for adding someone to your charity as a Charity Facilitator. Onboarding a facilitator is a two-step process: you invite them as a team member from your charity dashboard, and then a SafelyNested administrator adds the facilitator role to their account. This guide walks through both halves precisely.

Once promoted, a Charity Facilitator has the same Team-management powers as the primary contact — they can invite new team members, cancel pending invitations, and review the team roster.

What is a Charity Facilitator?

SafelyNested has two charity-side roles. Both belong to your organization, but they have different powers:

Standard team member
Charity Partner
Creates and manages referrals, invites participants, uploads documents, manages locations.
Elevated team member
Charity Facilitator
Everything a Charity Partner can do, plus access to the Facilitator Dashboard with charity-wide oversight tools.

A Charity Facilitator can do everything a Charity Partner can, plus access the Facilitator Dashboard with elevated views over the charity's referrals and bookings, and manage the charity's team (invite or cancel team members) alongside the primary contact.

Why two steps? Your team-invite form on the charity dashboard creates new accounts as Charity Partners by default. Promoting a user to Charity Facilitator requires a SafelyNested administrator. This is intentional — it keeps a second set of eyes on who gains elevated access.

Before you begin

PrerequisiteNotes
Your charity must already be approved on SafelyNested.If not, see the Onboarding a Charity guide first.

The new facilitator's email must use your charity's email domain.For example, if you registered with name@hopeshelter.org, all team invites must be to @hopeshelter.org addresses.

The 5-step facilitator onboarding flow

1

Primary contact or facilitator: open the Team page

Log in at safelynested.com/login as either the primary contact or an existing Charity Facilitator.

In the purple Charity Partner sidebar, click Team.

Important — where the link lives: The Team link only appears in the purple Charity Partner sidebar (the layout you land on by default after login, or via the Dashboard link). It does not appear on the green Facilitator Dashboard, which has its own minimal layout. If you're on the green page and don't see Team, click the regular Dashboard link to switch back to the purple side — the Team link will be there.

The Team page shows three things:

  • The invite form on the left.
  • Your current team members in a table on the right.
  • A history of pending and past invitations.

If you don't see the Team link from the purple sidebar at all, your account is neither the primary contact nor a Charity Facilitator — ask one of them to send the invitation, or contact your SafelyNested administrator.

2

Primary contact or facilitator: send the invitation

In the Invite Team Member card on the left, fill in:

  • Email Address * — must end in your charity's domain (the page shows the allowed domain in a blue badge).
  • First name and last name — helps the recipient recognize the email.
  • Personal Message — optional. A short note that appears in the invitation email above the "Create Your Account" button.

Click Send Invitation. The page shows a green "Sending invitation..." spinner while the email is dispatched, then returns you to the Team page with the new pending invite shown.

What just happened: An invitation email was sent and a registration link, valid for 7 days, was generated.
3

New team member: register their account

The recipient receives an email titled:

You've been invited to join {Your Charity} on SafelyNested

They click Create Your Account, which opens the registration form. Their email is pre-filled and read-only. They fill in:

  • Username *
  • Password * and Confirm Password *
  • First name, last name, phone (optional)
  • Agree to the Terms and Conditions *

They click Create Account, are redirected to the login page, and receive a welcome email with their username.

Important: At this point the new team member has the Charity Partner role — not yet Charity Facilitator. They can log in and use the standard charity features but cannot yet manage the team. The next step adds the elevated role; once promoted, they will see the Team link in their own sidebar and can invite future team members themselves.
4

Primary contact or existing facilitator: request the role upgrade

Once the team member has registered (you can confirm this on the Team page — their invite status will change from Pending to Accepted), contact your SafelyNested administrator and ask them to add the Charity Facilitator role to that user.

Provide:

  • Your charity name.
  • The new team member's username or email.
  • A brief reason (for example: "Will oversee booking approvals for our charity.").

The admin will edit the user record at /admin/users, check the CHARITY_FACILITATOR role on top of CHARITY_PARTNER, and save.

Note: The user keeps both roles. They retain everything a Charity Partner can do and gain Charity Facilitator access on top.
5

Facilitator: confirm access on next login

The next time the new facilitator logs in, they'll see a new menu item in the sidebar:

Facilitator Dashboard — highlighted in green and linked to /charity-facilitator/dashboard.

Clicking it opens the Charity Facilitator Dashboard. From here they can:

  • View charity-specific statistics.
  • Review referrals across the whole charity.
  • Manage bookings.

They can switch back to the Charity Partner Dashboard at any time using the regular Dashboard link in the sidebar. The Team link will appear there too — they now have full team-management powers alongside the primary contact.

Managing pending invitations

The lower half of the Team page lists every invitation you've ever sent, with its current status:

StatusMeaningWhat you can do
PendingThe invitee has not yet registered.Click Cancel to revoke the invitation if it was sent in error.
AcceptedThe invitee registered. They are now a team member.No action; they appear in the team members list above.
Expired7 days passed without registration.Send a fresh invitation.
CancelledYou cancelled the invite.None.

Common issues

ProblemWhat to do
The invite form rejects my colleague's email.Their address must end in your charity's allowed domain (shown in the blue badge). If they have a personal address (e.g. Gmail), they cannot be invited — this is an intentional security control. Contact your SafelyNested administrator if you need an exception.
The 7-day registration link expired.From the Team page, you can send a new invitation. (The old expired one cannot be reused.)
I can't see the Team menu item.Two things to check, in order: (1) Are you on the green Facilitator Dashboard? It has no sidebar — click Dashboard to switch to the purple Charity Partner side. (2) On the purple side, the Team link only appears for the primary contact and Charity Facilitators. If you're a regular Charity Partner, you won't see it — ask your primary contact or a facilitator to send the invitation instead.
The new facilitator logged in but doesn't see the Facilitator Dashboard.The role hasn't been added yet. Step 4 (admin promotion) is still pending. Have them log out and log back in after the admin confirms the change.

Help and support

The role-promotion step in particular requires a SafelyNested administrator and is not something charity staff can do themselves. If your administrator is slow to respond and your facilitator needs access urgently — for example, to handle bookings for families needing housing tonight — flag the urgency in your request.
